Projects

Using data obtained from various clubs at Florida Polytechnic University (club names, budgets, members, events), my project team and I created a database aimed at answering complex questions such as: club budget cost per student, student gender ratios in different clubs, and amount of student attendees per club event.

One of the main hurdles for this project was obtaining correct data. At the time of this project's conception, many clubs at the University did not keep sufficient data regarding regarding anything but budgeting and member information. Normalized club documentation only required an attendee count, resulting in any event student sign-in being temporary or lost.

Programmed in Java utilizing the JavaFX library, this individual assignment of a Monopoly recreation was styled with Florida Polytechnic University in mind. This Mono-Poly experience includes: animated graphics accurately rendering the precision movement of character pieces soullessly moving from one spot to another, a wide assortment of characters pieces to choose from- consisting of random close up pictures of my friends, and finally a semi-accurate representation of Monopoly's functionality. At Florida Polytechnic University, one of our core classes across all majors is the Capstone Project- a senior level course where groups of different studies work together on a single year-long project. For our project, we were tasked with developing an automatic citrus picker: a mobile unit capable of detecting ripe citrus along a groove, and running a set of motions to pick the citrus from the groove onto a basket. For this development project, the computer science team was concerned about two things: identifying a ripe citrus, and its distance from the camera.

Using Python and an Xbox 360 Kinect, we were able to corner those problems into a solution: the Kinect's infrared camera was used to determine distance, and a Python script was developed to detect objects by their shape and hue.
